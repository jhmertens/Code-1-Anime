What are good filmmakers to start with?

Hayao Miyazaki is one of anime's most renowned directors. He co-founded the international anime powerhouse Studio Ghibli and produced several influential texts including My Neighbor Totoro (1988), Princess Mononokie (1997), and the Academy Award winning Spirited Away (2001). He has technically retired from cinema twice but is currently making a final feature film for his studio.

Makoto Shinkai is currently poised for great success as a new and increasingly prominent anime director. He has built his career on both a fascination with digital lighting schemes and narratives that involve themes of nostalgia and loss. His work includes the Ghibli-esque fantasy Children Who Chase Lost Voice (2011) and the visually stunning Five Centimeters per Second (2007). More recently, his film Your Name. (2016) became an instant success and is currently the highest grossing anime film in cinematic history.

Satoshi Kon had a short but brilliant career but dying of colon cancer in 2010. His work often emphasized a playful relationship with time and space and the video essayist Tony Zhou demonstrated that in his excellent video found here. His most notable works were Perfect Blue (1997), Millennium Actress (2001), and Paprika (2006).
